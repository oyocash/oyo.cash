Search operators

The search field utilizes mongodb's $search operator. As result, the query syntax is the same. You can learn more about in mongodb's documentation: https://docs.mongodb.com/manual/reference/operator/query/text/#search-field

Search hashtags

Put # in front of a word and enclose the word in double quotes ("). For example: "#bitquery" [see on Oyo]

Exclude words from your search

Put - in front of a word you want to leave out. For example, bitcoin -core [see on Oyo]

Search protocol prefixes

Put prefix: in front of a prefix code. For example, prefix:6d02 cats [see on Oyo]

Search in apps

Put app: in front of the app identifier. For example, app:twetch love [see on Oyo]

Search from date

Put datetime: in front of date time (format: yyyyMMddHHmm). For example, prefix:status datetime:201904010000 [see on Oyo]

Search for period

The default period is 30 days.

Put period: in front of the desired search period (in days). For example, prefix:status datetime:201907010000 period:90 [see on Oyo]

Search transactions from (sender)

Put from: in front of the sender's address. For example from:17yvo2MY6DSX4JFJNWzEdav95EDE9PcWvx bitdb [see on Oyo]

Search transactions excluding sender

Put notfrom: in front of the sender's address. For example notfrom:17yvo2MY6DSX4JFJNWzEdav95EDE9PcWvx bitdb [see on Oyo]

Exact match

Put a word or phrase inside quotes. For example, "bitcoin unlimited". [see on Oyo]

Combining searches

Put a word or phrase inside quotes for mandatory match (AND). Use a word without quotes for optional match (OR). Examples:
"moneybutton" "wordpress" (searches for "moneybutton AND wordpress") [see on Oyo]
liquid lightning (searches for "liquid OR lightning") [see on Oyo]
"market" bull bear (searches for "market AND (market OR bull OR bear)") [see on Oyo]
